“The Global Auto Industry Faces a Reckoning”

Many established automakers are retreating from bold electric and digital transformation plans amid rising competition and shifting strategy. Stellantis, for example, is grappling with major losses and scrapped goals. Meanwhile, China’s BYD dominates with strong battery technology, export reach, and consumer features like over-the-air upgrades. Ford shines through new service-based revenue models.

“US EV Sales Surge as Buyers Race to Beat Expiring Tax Credit”

In August 2025, U.S. electric vehicle (EV) sales are estimated to hit a record 12.8% of total auto sales, driven by a rush before the $7,500 federal EV tax credit ends at the end of September. However, analysts caution that this spike may be temporary, expecting a sharp slowdown afterward.

“China’s EV Price War: New Players Disrupting the Market”

In recent years, nimble players like Leapmotor, Xiaomi, and Xpeng are outpacing traditional giants like BYD in China’s EV market. Leapmotor posted its first profit, Xiaomi cut losses and enjoyed a 70% rise in overall profit, while Xpeng significantly reduced its losses. Many of these competitors are licensing technology to global automakers.
